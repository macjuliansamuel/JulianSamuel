Background

The government estimates that up to 176 million young people in India are in need of care and protection. Many of these students don’t have access to positive role models, cannot envision a career, and don’t feel connected to their communities. It is tragic that young people are much more likely to be unemployed or live in poverty. Mentoring creates meaningful connections that can positively impact the lives of both mentor and mentee.

Overview

The Problem

“I wanted to create a mentoring platform because being a mentee I always found it hard to keep track of my tasks and schedule assigned by my mentor. This problem is particularly challenging since there aren’t a lot of platforms that cater to the needs of a mentor and mentee”

The Solution

I used design thinking methodologies to create a mobile application that acts as an interface between mentor and mentee. It allows the mentee to stay focused on the goals and the mentor to track the progress made by the mentee in real-time.

"How to make the interaction between a mentor and mentee simple"

Research

We conducted a series of user interviews to figure out the process of mentoring and pain points that the users felt.

Relevant Findings

Mentoring Flow

Mentee had to schedule a meet with the mentor
The mentor schedules a meet with mentor,

MENTORING SESSION
The mentoring session involves encouraging the mentee, planning long term goals and assigning tasks for mentee to complete before the next session.

MENTEE WRITES A REPORT OF THE MEETING
The mentee writes a minutes of the meeting to the mentor

MENTEE SETS WEEKLY TASKS AND GOALS
The mentee writes a minutes of the meeting to the mentor. The minutes of the meeting contains everything that's been discussed along with a action plan ( Goals & Tasks ) for the next week.

MENTEE SENDS A REPORT OF THE PROGRESS MADE
At the end of the end of the week, the mentee sends a report of the tasks completed.

MENTOR GIVES HIS FEEDBACK AND PLAN FOR NEXT WEEK
In the next session the mentor analyzes the mentor progress made by the student and gives his feedback. The plan for the next week is also carried.

Inital Sketches

Once we organized all our research and defined our solution, we began to explore potential designs for the website.

Lo-fi Prototype

Scheduling Tasks

The plus icon on the top of every screen lets the users add new task, goals or meeting. Tapping the plus brings down a list of options to choose from. Tapping on the icon redirects to the appropriate screen

Completing Task

Task can be marked complete by swiping. Swiping it left brings up two options, one to mark the task as complete and the other two delete the task. Once the task is marked complete it updates the stats page.

Hi-fi Prototype

Onboarding

The onboarding process allows the users to know more about the program and how it works, It also has feedback from the alumnus of the program to increase the credibility of the program. The last few slides let the user know what to expect from the app. While doing the the user gets familiarized with the app’s interface.

Home Page

The Home Page displayed all the tasks, goals and meetings in a card-based format. The cards are arranged in a chronological order. Each card contains a description of the task, finish time and a label. The tasks has additional information that shows the goal the task is related to. The Goals card has a badge that indicates the status of the goal

Stats and Achievemnets

The stats page displays the progress and achievements made my by the mentee over the course of the program. The bar graph shows the progress made by the student over the selected span of time while the circular chart shows the current status of the targeted goals.
The badges are achieved by the user once an goal is complete. The badeges induces a sense of gamification and keeps the user focused on the goals.
